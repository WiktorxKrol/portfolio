## Owning four products at the same time

At peak I was running four products in parallel. Each one was at a different stage. Each one needed a different way of working.

**BOWWE** - mature SaaS website and e-commerce builder. Active e-commerce release in progress, live bugs to triage, sprint close-outs with hard deadlines. This one needed strict hygiene - clear ownership per bug, clear sprint boundaries, no rolling work forward without tracking it.

**Honaro** - service marketplace. I was adding new features alongside a UI refresh. One example: electronic vouchers and a loyalty stamp system - users collect stamps for visits, get rewarded for coming back. CEO's idea, I scoped it from scratch. Honaro is a more stable product with a smaller active surface, so it ran with lighter process overhead than BOWWE.

**Proptech app** - being scoped from scratch. No sprints yet, no development. The rhythm here was one epic scoped and reviewed per session with the investor. Pushing faster would have meant pushing incomplete scope to engineering.

**TaskRabbit integration** - three teams, fixed external deadline. No sprint structure works here. The bottleneck is always on the other side, so the job was daily follow-up on every blocking item and keeping a visible status across all three teams.

---

**The mistake would have been treating them the same.**

Daily standups on a scoping-phase product waste everyone's time. Epic-by-epic checkpoints on a live release miss bugs. I applied different cadences on purpose, not because I couldn't manage them consistently, but because the products needed different things.

The result: all four kept moving. No product went quiet because it wasn't the current focus. Stakeholders across all four had a current picture of status without me running a catch-up meeting every week.

---

**Skills:** multi-product ownership, process design per product stage, prioritization under competing deadlines, stakeholder communication.
