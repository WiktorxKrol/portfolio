## Owning four products at different stages simultaneously

> **In one line:** I ran product decisions across four parallel efforts at the same time - a mature SaaS product mid-release, a second marketplace product, a brand-new app in scoping, and a cross-company integration on a hard deadline - without letting any of them stall.

**Situation.** At peak, I was responsible for four concurrent efforts: BOWWE (mature SaaS, active e-commerce release with live bugs), Honaro (service marketplace, ongoing feature work), a new proptech app being scoped from scratch, and a cross-company integration with a fixed external deadline. Each one required a different operating rhythm. Treating them all the same would have broken at least two of them.

**What I did.**

- **BOWWE:** Strict sprint hygiene. Tracked bugs per engineer, kept a clear view of overdue work and ownership, coordinated release close-outs with hard deadlines.
- **Honaro:** Feature-focused sprints with lighter process overhead - a more mature, stable product with a smaller surface area of active change.
- **Proptech app:** Epic-by-epic scoping with investor checkpoints. No sprints yet - this was pre-development. The rhythm was one epic scoped and reviewed per session.
- **Cross-company integration:** Daily follow-up on external dependencies. No sprint structure works here - the bottleneck is always on the other side, so the job is to keep every blocking item visible and chased.

Applied different cadences deliberately. The mistake would have been to impose the same process everywhere - daily standups on a scoping-phase product waste time, and epic-by-epic checkpoints on a live release miss bugs.

**Result.** All four efforts moved forward concurrently. No product went quiet because it was not the current focus. Stakeholders across all four had a clear, up-to-date picture of status without me needing to run a single all-hands catch-up.

**Skills:** multi-product ownership, process design, prioritization under competing deadlines, stakeholder communication, sprint management.
